# Methodology

This document explains what People's Voices is, what it represents, and — equally importantly — what it does **not** represent.

---

> **Disclaimer:** This platform visualizes voluntary submissions from individuals who choose to participate. It is intended to document participation and perspectives, not to estimate public opinion or represent the views of the general population.

---

## 📌 What This Platform Does

People's Voices is a **civic participation documentation tool**. It allows individuals to:

- Voluntarily share their perspective on civic matters
- Indicate their city and state of participation
- Have their voice represented on an interactive map alongside others

The platform aggregates these voluntary submissions to create a visual representation of:

- **Where** people are participating
- **What** perspectives participants are sharing
- **How** participation is distributed across Indian cities and states

---

## 🚫 What This Platform Does NOT Represent

### Not a Poll or Survey

People's Voices is **not** a scientific poll, survey, or public opinion measurement tool. Key differences:

| Scientific Poll | People's Voices |
|---|---|
| Random sampling of population | Self-selected participants |
| Controlled methodology | Open, voluntary submissions |
| Statistical margin of error | No statistical claims |
| Representative of population | Representative only of participants |
| Designed to estimate public opinion | Designed to document participation |

### Not a Census

This platform does not attempt to count or enumerate any population. The numbers shown represent **submissions received**, not total participants or supporters.

### Not Representative of General Public Opinion

The data visualized on this platform comes exclusively from individuals who chose to visit and submit. It cannot and should not be extrapolated to represent the views of:

- The general Indian population
- Any specific demographic group
- Any geographic region as a whole
- Any political party or organization

## ⚠️ Self-Selection Bias

### What Is Self-Selection Bias?

Self-selection bias occurs when individuals choose whether or not to participate in a study or platform. Those who participate may differ systematically from those who do not.

### How It Applies Here

- **Only people who are aware of the platform** can participate
- **Only people with internet access** can submit
- **Only people who feel motivated** to share will do so
- **People in areas with higher social media reach** may be overrepresented
- **Urban areas** may show more participation than rural areas due to digital access

### What This Means

The participation data should be interpreted as: *"Among people who chose to participate on this platform, here is what they shared and where they are located."*

It should **never** be interpreted as: *"This is what the people of [city/state/India] think."*

## 🙋 Voluntary Participation

### Fully Voluntary

- No one is required to submit
- No one is incentivized to submit
- No accounts or sign-ups are required
- Participants choose what to share and from which location

### Anonymous

- No personal information is collected (see [Privacy Policy](./PRIVACY.md))
- Submissions cannot be traced back to any individual
- There is no way to verify the identity or location of any participant

### Implications

Because participation is voluntary and anonymous:

- The same person could theoretically submit multiple times
- The stated location cannot be independently verified
- The sincerity of submissions cannot be guaranteed

The platform relies on good faith participation and applies content moderation to filter out clearly inappropriate submissions.

---

## 🛡️ Moderation

### Content Moderation

- Submissions are filtered for profanity using automated tools
- Content that is hateful, threatening, or clearly spam is filtered
- The moderation system aims to preserve genuine voices while preventing abuse

### What Moderation Does NOT Do

- Moderation does not verify the truthfulness of submissions
- Moderation does not assess the political alignment of submissions
- Moderation does not filter based on viewpoint or opinion

## 🔓 Open Source

### Full Transparency

People's Voices is **fully open source** under the [MIT License](./LICENSE):

- All source code is publicly available and auditable
- The data processing pipeline is transparent
- Anyone can inspect how data is collected, stored, and displayed
- Anyone can verify that no hidden tracking or data collection occurs

### Why Open Source Matters

- **Trust**: Users can verify the platform's privacy claims by reading the code
- **Accountability**: The community can identify and report issues
- **Transparency**: No hidden algorithms, no black boxes, no undisclosed data collection
- **Reproducibility**: Anyone can fork the project and run their own instance

## 📊 How to Interpret the Data

### ✅ Appropriate Interpretations

- "X number of submissions were received from people indicating they are in [city]"
- "Among participants, these were the common themes"
- "Participation on this platform was distributed across these regions"
- "The platform received submissions from X cities across Y states"

### ❌ Inappropriate Interpretations

- "X% of people in [city] support [cause]"
- "The majority of Indians believe..."
- "People in [state] think that..."
- "This data proves that public opinion is..."
